Life on the Track

Around 8 weeks greyhound pups are weaned from their mothers. Usually they remain with their litter mates on a greyhound farm where they chase, run, and play. At 5 months they are paired off, and spend the next 7 months in serious training which includes leash training, learning voice commands, and getting to know a muzzle. At 1 year old greyhounds are moved to a training facility near a race track. Here they have a disciplined schedule and learn to exercise and run against other hounds. At 14 months greyhounds begin training on the track twice a week. Between 15 and 18 months greyhounds move to the track and train there as well. There they have their first race, a non-betting preliminary. If they do well in the preliminary, they move on to their maiden race. At this point if the greyhound has not raced well, they are retired.

On the track, greyhounds usually race twice a week. The rest of the time they spend in their 3ft x 5ft kennel. It's estimated that greyhounds on the track are kenneled 21/24 hours a day. Often dogs are muzzled while kenneled.
